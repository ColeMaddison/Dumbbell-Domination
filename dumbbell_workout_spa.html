Day 1: Upper Body Push & Pull Power

Equipment: Two 10kg dumbbells. Load one to 18kg for Single-Arm Rows.

1. Dumbbell Floor Press

Chest & Triceps

  • Sets: 4
  • Reps: 12-15
  • Tempo: 3-1-1-0

Lie on your back, knees bent, feet flat. Press two 10kg dumbbells straight up from your chest.

2. Single-Arm Dumbbell Rows

Back & Biceps

  • Weight: 18kg
  • Sets: 4
  • Reps: 8-12 / arm
  • Tempo: 3-0-1-0

Place one hand and knee on a bench/chair, back flat. Row the dumbbell towards your hip, squeezing your shoulder blade.

3. Dumbbell Flyes

Chest

  • Sets: 3
  • Reps: 15-20
  • Tempo: 3-0-1-0

Lie on your back, arms extended above chest with a slight bend in elbows. Lower two 10kg dumbbells out to the sides in an arc.

4. Standing Overhead Press

Shoulders & Triceps

  • Sets: 3
  • Reps: 10-15
  • Tempo: 2-0-1-0

Stand tall, core braced, dumbbells at shoulder height. Press two 10kg dumbbells overhead.

5. Superset: Curls & Extensions

Biceps & Triceps

  • Sets: 3
  • Reps: 12-15 each
  • Tempo: 3-0-1-0

Perform Bicep Curls (2x 10kg) then immediately Overhead Triceps Extensions (1x 10kg).

6. Renegade Rows

Core & Upper Body

  • Sets: 3
  • Reps: 8-10 / arm
  • Tempo: Controlled

Plank with hands on two 10kg dumbbells. Row one up to your side, keeping your body stable. Alternate arms.

  • Reps: 15-20

Day 7: Active Recovery / Rest

Today is about letting your body heal and prepare for the next week. Rest is not a sign of weakness; it's a critical part of getting stronger. Choose an option that feels best for you.

  • →Light Cardio: A brisk 30-45 minute walk or light cycle.
  • →Stretching & Mobility: Focus on static stretches (holding 20-30s) and mobility drills.
  • →Yoga/Pilates: A gentle session to improve flexibility and core stability.
  • →Complete Rest: If you feel truly fatigued, take a full day off. Listen to your body.

Key Principles for Intensity

Because the absolute weight is limited, we manufacture intensity through technique. This is how we make light weights feel heavy and drive progress.

  • Tempo Training: Control the speed of every rep (e.g., 3 seconds lowering, 1 second lifting). This increases time under tension, a key driver for muscle growth.
  • High Repetitions: Working in higher rep ranges ($12-20+$) pushes muscular endurance and creates a metabolic environment conducive to growth.
  • Short Rest Periods: Resting only 30-60 seconds between sets keeps your heart rate elevated and muscles working, increasing the overall challenge.
  • Unilateral Work: Training one limb at a time forces your core to work harder to stabilize your body, turning every exercise into a core exercise.
  • Isometric Holds: Pausing at the most difficult part of a lift (e.g., the bottom of a squat) eliminates momentum and forces your muscles to work harder.

Warm-up & Cool-down

Never skip these. A proper warm-up prevents injury and prepares your body for work. A cool-down aids recovery and improves flexibility.

Warm-up (5-10 minutes)

  • Light cardio (jumping jacks, high knees)
  • Dynamic stretches (arm circles, leg swings, torso twists)
  • Light dumbbell presses and arm circles to activate the target muscles.

Cool-down (5-10 minutes)

  • Static stretches for all major muscle groups worked.
  • Hold each stretch for 20-30 seconds. Do not bounce.

How to Progress

To keep getting stronger, you must consistently challenge yourself. This is called Progressive Overload. Since you can't always add more weight, use these methods:

  • Increase Reps: Once you can comfortably complete the target reps for all sets, aim for 1-2 more reps per set next time.
  • Increase Sets: Add an extra set to one or two exercises in your workout.
  • Reduce Rest: Shave 5-10 seconds off your rest periods between sets.
  • Slow Down Tempo: Increase the eccentric (lowering) phase to 4 or 5 seconds.
  • Add Holds: Add a 2-3 second pause at the most challenging point of the exercise.
